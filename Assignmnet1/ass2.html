Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being “the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world’s heritage”. It is regarded by many as the best example of Mughal architecture and a symbol of India’s rich history1. The construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. It took a total of twenty-two years to complete the construction of this monument

Machu Pichu

Machu Picchu is believed to have been constructed as an estate for the Inca emperor Pachacuti (1438–1472) and was occupied from c. 1420–15321. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World2. The Incas built the estate around 1450 but abandoned it a century later, at the time of the Spanish conquest1. Machu Picchu is located about 50 miles (80 km) northwest of Cuzco, Peru, in the Cordillera de Vilcabamba of the Andes Mountains3. The site was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is a ruined ancient Maya city occupying an area of 4 square miles (10 square km) in south-central Yucatán state, Mexico. It is thought to have been a religious, military, political, and commercial center that at its peak would have been home to 35,000 people

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, generally built along an east-to-west line across the historical northern borders of China to protect against various nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppe. The wall is 21,196 kilometers long1 and is more than 2700 years old today

Petra

Petra is one of the oldest cities in the world. Archaeologists believe the Nabateans established the city in 312 BC. It was the capital city of Nabateans, an ancient Arab tribe who inhabited northern Arabia and the southern Levant and arrived in Jordan around the 6th century BC1. Petra was voted one of the New 7 Wonders of the World in 20072. The entrance to Petra is the Siq, a narrow gorge around 1km long2. Petra’s centerpiece is the Treasury or Al-Khazneh, a 40 meters high monument with a stunning façade2.

Colossus of Rome

The Colosseum was built between 72 A.D and 80 A.D under the Emperor Vespasian, in the heart of Ancient Rome. It is made from stone and concrete, this magnificent monument was built with the man power of tens of thousands of slaves1. The Colosseum was the largest amphitheater in the Roman world measuring some 620 by 513 feet (190 by 155 meters)2. It was a freestanding structure made of stone and concrete2

Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex created by the ancient Egyptians. It was built as a tomb for the pharaoh Khufu and was completed around 2560 BCE. It is one of the oldest and most recognizable monuments in the world and is still considered one of the most remarkable engineering feats in history.

The Great Pyramid of Giza is made up of an estimated 2.3 million limestone blocks and has a base width of over 230 meters. Its height was originally 146 meters but has since decreased to 138 meters due to weathering.
