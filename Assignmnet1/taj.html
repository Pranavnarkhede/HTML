Taj Mahal


  • The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, India.
  • It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.
  • Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653.
  • The Taj Mahal is made of white marble that was sourced from various parts of India.
  • The intricate carvings and calligraphy on the Taj Mahal are made of semi-precious stones and were done by skilled artisans.
  • The four minarets surrounding the Taj Mahal were built tilted outwardly, so that in case of an earthquake they would fall away from the main structure and not towards it.
  • The Taj Mahal is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world and attracts millions of visitors every year.


The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is considered to be one of the greatest architectural achievements in human history.
